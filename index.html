01 — the weight you carry

The weight
you carry
alone.

Every morning you open a textbook you didn't choose. Every night you carry the silence of words you never got to say.

The pressure isn't just the syllabus. It lives in the way someone looks at you when you don't have an answer. In the unspoken fear of becoming a disappointment.

"She's studying so hard." — but nobody asked if she was okay.

You've been strong for so long. For everyone else. But inside, something quieter in you is waiting — waiting to be heard, waiting to be real.

That tiredness you feel? It's not weakness. It's the exhaustion of becoming someone you never agreed to be.

The child
inside you
